About Babbage

  • Born in Walworth, Surrey, on December 26, 1791
  • One of four children born to the banker Benjamin Babbage and Elizabeth Teape
  • Attended Trinity, Cambridge, in 1810 to study mathematics
  • Graduated without honors from Peterhouse in 1814
  • Received an MA in 1817
  • Married to Georgiana Whitmore in 1814 with whom he had eight children, only three of whom lived to adulthood
  • His wife, father, and two of his children died in 1827
  • In 1828 Babbage moved to 1 Dorset Street, Marylebone, which remained his home till his death
  • He was elected as a fellow of the Royal Society in 1816
  • Occupied the Lucasian chair of mathematics at Cambridge University from 1828 to 1839
  • He died on October 18, 1871 and was buried at Kensal Green cemetery in London
